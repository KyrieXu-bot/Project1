Yu Shan Hill----The seven streams flow to the sea, and ten miles of green hills half enter the city.

Yushan, located in Changshu City, Jiangsu Province, was called Wumu Mountain in the past, lying on the northwest of Changshu City, adjacent to the Yangtze River in the north, and Shanghu Lake in the south. It is a national AAAA scenic spot.


It was named after the ancestor Yu Zhong (Zhong Yong) of Jiangnan, who was buried here after the death of Shang and Zhou Dynasties. The southeast foot of Yushan extends into the ancient city, so it has the reputation of "a half-a-century into the city". Yushan Mountain is divided into Yushan Park and Yushan National Forest Park. Yushan Park is part of the Liangshan Project in Changshu City.


The mountain of Yushan spreads from northwest to southeast, with rolling peaks and ridges, an altitude of 263 meters, a width of about 3 kilometers from north to south, a length of about 7 kilometers from east to west, and a circle around the foot of about 20 kilometers. Yushan is located in the northwest part of the ancient city of Changshu, halfway into the ancient city, and is known as Wumu Mountain, Haiyu Mountain and Haiwu Mountain. It is like a lying cow, also known as a lying cow. Why is it called Yushan? It is related to the sage of the country, Zhong Yong. Zhong Yong, also called Yu Zhong, was the son of an ancient father in the late Shang Dynasty more than three thousand years ago.


Yushan winding southeast into the city is the tomb of the ancestor of the Wu culture, Zhongyong Yong, the tomb of Zhouzhang, the first monarch of the ancient Wu Kingdom, and the tomb of Yanzi, the ancestor of the southeast culture and one of the 72 wise disciples of Confucius. Shanmen, Prince Liang Zhaoming's Xiaotong reading desk, the northern and mountain tops include Xingfu Temple, Weimo Mountain Villa, Gujian Pavilion, Zanghai Temple and other tombs of the Ming Dynasty national hero Qu Shiyun, as well as the tomb of the great painter Huang Gongwang of the Yuan Dynasty, the Ming Dynasty Tomb of Wang Fu, a famous anti-Japanese warrior, Qian Qianyi, the first generation of Emperor Wenzong of the Ming and Qing Dynasties, tomb of courtesan Liu Rushi, the great painter of the Qing Dynasty, the tomb of Wang Shigu who created the "Yushan School of Painting" Tomb of Weng Tonghe in the Reformation, Tomb of famous writer Zeng Pu in the early Qing Dynasty.


Yushan integrates with the ancient city and Shangnan Lake, forming a unique landscape feature. The natural scenery is beautiful, the cultural landscape is rich, and it has always been a tourist destination in Jiangnan. Yushan Monk Lake constitutes Yushan Scenic Area, covering an area of ​​40 square kilometers. In 1982, it was listed as one of the scenic spots of the National Taihu Lake Scenic Spot, and at the same time it was listed as a key scenic spot in Jiangsu Province. It consists of 6 scenic spots including Xinfeng, Weimo, Xingfu, Jianmen, Xiaoshidong and Shanghu.
